New Features

      Even though CONITEC includes in each new release a documentation of the new features, we're asked quite frequently how one or the other feature may be used to good effect. So here are a few tips and suggestions from the WDL kitchen.

1. Source of light

    As yet there is no "Shadow-Mapping" i.e. real time computing of light sources and shadows. But there is a way to circumvent the annoying need for multiple definition of wall textures with different Ambients: Albedo, Lightangle and Genius.

    Albedo is a texture flag allowing you define a limit between 0 and 1 concerning the variation of ambient An Albedo is defined as follows:

      TEXTURE Wand_tex {SCALE_XY 16,16; BMAPS wand_map; ALBEDO 0.3; }

    If in the head defintions of the WDL a LIGHT_ANGLE was set beforehand, the wall will now be lightened up or darkened depending on the angle ANGLE'. The wall texture of a column for example would now be darkened on the side pointing away from the angle, producing a nice 3D effect. LIGHT_ANGLE is definded in Radiant, thus for east:

      LIGHT_ANGLE 0;

    Additionally a so called GENIUS may be set for the respective region. A GENIUS is not a math coprocessor, but a simple ACTOR with a transparent, pixelsized texture. It could also be a real torch, or a fluorescent daisy, if you like. The ACTOR is defined like any other:

      ACTOR Genius_Wirtshaus_im_Spessart {TEXTURE blacktex;}

    And afterwards you assign the Genius to the region:

      REGION gaststube {
      FLOOR_HGT 0;
      CEIL_HGT 22;
      FLOOR_TEX holzdiele_tex;
      CEIL_TEX holzcassette_tex;
      GENIUS Genius_Wirtshaus_im_Spessart;
      }

    Now all you have to do is place the actor in the WED. Possibly where the light is supposed to come from. That's all. You may of course assign this actor to as many adjacent regions as you like, e.g. Region_Theke, Region_Kneipentisch, Region_Separee, etc. It then "shines" across borders, so to say. Because obviously a source of light is there for the whole pub.

2. CD-AUDIO

    Usually there are two ways to play music: using a WAVE file or a MIDI file. WAVE files are not recommended, because they use up huge amounts of memory. A "sampled" song of one minute duration needs 1 MB in the 11 khz format already, the 22 khz format even needs 2 MB. That is why such WAVE files are only produced for use in small levels containing the opening credits, because for a complete game level one minute of music will never be enough. Thus usually General Midi (GM) files are used. Here the song is plaed in real time by the respective sound card in FM qualitiy. This usually sounds awful, unless you are one of the lucky few who can call a Roland Wavetable their own (even a AWE32 still sounds rather unimpressive). Additionally you will find that seperate sound banks (IBK's) need to be produced for playing FM - a rather dire and thankless job.

    Now you may also play a song directly from a CD. For that, the CD player must be wired to the sound card internally or you can listen to the music with a headphone connected to the headphone outlet of the CD player. CD audio does of course only work after finishing loading the level completely, as the CD player can only then access the audio files on the CD. The quality of sound is then only limited by the abilities of modern sound mastering. Theoretically you may right away introduce Bruckner's TE DEUM as played by the Berlin Philharmonics under Karajan, or the original soundtrack for STAR WARS. All you need is a single command, which may be called up at your will during START ACTION or whenever, just like the PLAY_SONG command for Midi files:

      PLAY_CD 1,10;

    With the first parameter indicatingthe number and position of the respecitve audio track and the second parameter giving the maximum number of tracks on the disk. The only drawback of CD_AUDIO:

    • Access time increases the further the write/read head of the CD-Player is from the audio track
    • The user will have to make sure that his sound card is wired to the CD player.
    • The game programmer has to go to a studio and does no longer have any excuses, if the music is no good......
