No, I didn't! It's just that I found out something VERY INTERESTING and I want to share it with you. Believe it or not, Acknex commercial works with models! Please sit back. It works with models, but shows them as wireframes. This way, you won't have to spend nights capturing hundreds of pcxs for a monster. Simply create a mdl monster, place it in your game and use it. When you have the money, buy the pro version and that's all. The mdl monsters respond to IF_HIT actions and other actions like they'd have skins. I was so curious to know what would J.C.Lotter about this. Here's his answer:

The commercial version supports models and shows them as wireframes. This was intended, and will stay as a feature within all future version too.

Thanks a lot, J.C.L.

I know you want to jump to your mdl actors now, so here's how easy it is to define one of my mdl actors:

 
MODEL model01, <m1.mdl>;

TEXTURE walk_tex {
MODEL model01;
DELAY 2; // animation delay
FRAME 1; // starts at model's frame 1
CYCLES 8; // has 8 frames
AMBIENT 0.1;
SOUND walk_snd;
SCYCLE 1;
SVOL 0.2;
SDIST 40;
}


TEXTURE run_tex {
MODEL model01;
DELAY 2;
FRAME 9;
CYCLES 8;
AMBIENT -0.3;
SOUND run_snd
SCYCLE 1;
SVOL 0.2;
SDIST 40;
}
