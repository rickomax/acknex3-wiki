INTERVIEW

Remi Valantin from Phaestos did answer our questions about PIKTO by email:

 

 

Q 1: What is the meaning of the word "PHAESTOS"?

A: Phaestos is the place where a disc has been found. Both sides of this clay disc have hieroglyphic characters separately impressed by means of punches and arranged in a spiral. The script has not yet been deciphered. About 1600 BC. Maybe so many years ago, they had already discovered the cdrom...

You will find herewith the logo of our company which is the clay disc.

Q 2: When was the company established and what are the targets of PHAESTOS?

A:You will find all the information about the company on www.phaestos.com (english pages).

Q 3: Your ususal business is it to make TV produktions. What kind are they?

A: All kind of productions : documentaries, news, reports.... see also our web site, you will find many information.

Q 4: How did it come to the idea to produce a VR-Game?

A: Hmm... it's a long story... Nicolas Eon is the author of the Pikto character. Pikto was originally an artwork on wood or big canvas painted by Nicolas.

Two years before, Phaestos produced a serial of short cartoons with Pikto, helped by Francois Rouquette, his energy and his links with Canal J. We used 3D studio to animate Pikto (yes, the old 3d studio 4 !). We were three animators to work on this series during three months - 140 episodes have been broadcasted on the cable channel, Canal J. After the end of this production, we made a level test with the Duke editor (Build) but with the fresh colored graphics of Nicolas and it's favorite character Pikto. Canal J was so enthusiastic by this test that we started to search for a more appropriate (and cheaper) engine.

Q 5: How did you become aware of ACKNEX?

A: Internet (vive Internet!). We tried many tools (of course at that time, it was hard to find a lot of engines) and Acknex seemed to me really interesting.

Q 6: What are the reasons to decide on this engine?

A: - Simple programming language, compared to a 3D API library.
- Affordable product: we could try Acknex without financial troubles.
- The features of the software seemed to suit our needs. Nevertheless, we had to make Conitec develop a lot of new features. And today Acknex users can benefit from these enhancements.

Q 7: How did your team look like, working on Pikto? How many people where in the team and who did what?

A: The basic team was :
- 2 authors : François and Nicolas (Artwork Director).
- 1 project manager
- 5 artists (graphics)
- 2 modelers (level designers)
- 2 programmers
- 1 musician + sound creator.

Q 8: What experiences did you gain working with the ACKNEX?

A: We had to perform a double task: the TV game show and the CD-ROM during only 10 months. It would be very long and hard to tell you all the story. One thing is for sure: nobody in the team had any experience in game developement. We had to learn everything, from organizing the work to the final testing. I do not think we could have managed this challenge without such an easy tool as Acknex. I must add that we learned (a little) to work on a game, but the most  difficult was to learn to work in a 'team spirit'...hmm... you understand that I can't speak about all...

Q 9: What advantages offer this engine in your opinion compared to others?

A: Main advantage: very easy to use. We did not have to care about the program code. Nothing to do with sound management, video, memory... If this engine had not been such an easy tool, we could not have involved in the middle of the project more new developers to get some help.

Q 10: What are the disadvantages? What could be improved?

A: A TV game MUST be in 640x480, and without PII processors, we could not have gotten a smooth frame rate. The editor is easy, efficient, but quite rough. Our modelors were afraid when they had to work on the attached textures on the walls.

The neverending enhancements of the engine during the first 7 months caused us many troubles. Sometimes we thought things were OK. The day after, the brand new beta WED brought us new problems.We had to test everything.

Of course it was necessary to improve the engine. But we would have appreciated more documentations of the news and the modifications.

The good side of this situation is that sometimes, when things were wrong, when something did not work, we could make some jokes as : "Have you tried it with the new WED??"

Q 11: How was the cooperation with Conitec?

A: A lot of emails! Generally, the cooperation was good and answers about our technicals questions and new features were quick. Johann Lotter's nick name was (for us): Mr. "No problem...". Of course, few times, I spend lot of hours (days !) before understanding that a bug comes from a beta release of the engine and not from my code, and then... oh no, I can't say what I thought then... but nobody is perfect, no? Well, the more often it was our  fault, WDL is perhaps simple, but not simplistic.

Q 12: When was Pikto released?

A: February 1998.

Q 13: Pikto was also released in Japan. Which are the next countries, can we hope for a release in Germany, USA and the rest of the world?

A: In fact Pikto will be released in Japan in few months (we've just finished the Win 95 adptation of the game), and there are some deals in progress with other european distributors.

Q 14: Is there a successor of Pikto, you are working on?

A: No, not a CD-ROM game at this time, just a new TV game in september.

Q 15: Are there further projects you are planning using the ACKNEX?

A: I have at least 3 personnal projects, but two of them are multiplayer internet games, so I'm waiting for the next release!

Q 16: Is there anything else, you want the visitors of this page to know?

A: Just a little thing, I don't think that Pikto is an unique game. After all, it is seen as a 'Duke' for children (and children really like it !), even if we tried to add some little new things to this kind of game. But, please, Acknex developpers, stop to try to imitate Duke, Quake and so on. They are many things much more unique to do with Acknex...

Q: Wait... at least there is some more question, we forgot in the interview: who, in the hell is Pikto?

A: Well, indeed, it's really simple, Pikto is 'ANYONE -EVERYBODY'. It's a
symbolic character who could become what you (hmm... the author) want.
It's more a concept of character than a character, in fact it's a
non-character, with some long hair, it's a woman (or a rock-star with a
guitar), with different clothes, it's a cowboy or a worker, with different
format of head and body it became a children or an adult etc...
