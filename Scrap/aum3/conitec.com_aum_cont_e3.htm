CONTEST '97/'98

I would like to extend many, if belated, thanks to all contributers! Still regardless of the thank-yous one thing should be clear henceforth: sending in diskettes with a few empty rooms and/or an extensive description of what may have could been but could not right now not yet and so on and so forth only costs your money for postage and our time. So please postpone sending in your stuff until it has grown beyond the "look here, my first room" stage.  

But now quickly for the winners of our second contest! To find out the three best was an easy task. They simply are the best - leaving the rest far behind them. But after that it was a close race and we had a hard time to decide on a ranking. So we settled for giving the ACKNEX professional version to all three of them. Nevertheless we do have, as is seemly, a third, second, and first place.

 

 

and the winner is...

SHADES by LIFE is or number 1!


Why? Because here finally came one that is not another XXX clone. It contains the greatest number of - well executed - ideas! There's no shooting here. But there is a parachute mode and a subway ride functioning smoothly. And before entering the sacred halls you can take a peek inside them through one of the several surveillance cameras. 

Topographically the level is neat. And those sounds that are included are of a high quality and create athmosphere. 

Those eager to nitpick will pick out the sometimes rather pixely graphics, the scanty fittings and a few more aesthetic shortcomings. And to be honest, we as well soon switched off the music... 

The guys from Life have not only shown creativity but also that they are able to use the engine, by including complex and varied WDL action. Which made them the winners! 

 

 

Platz 2...

The resplendent ISMAIN by Lutz Hüls!

ISMAIN already is a self-contained and completely playable level. There is shooting here, but at least this time what ist shot - for a change to wit! - is a mushroom tincture.  The level has a very beautiful topography. The quality sounds add to that and complete the athmospheric ambience. This entry boasts four different opponents.


The player as well as one of the opponents are shooting with specially programmed projectiles. So here as well complex programming was carried out, which, by the way, was documented in an exemplary way. 

Nitpickers may again pick on the sometimes pixely graphics. They might be pissed off because pointing and hitting is not always an easy task. But it is our opinion, that, given the character of this contest, this is still within the range of being acceptable. 
Lutz Hüls by the way produced all this using the smallest and oldest version of the engine. Which gets him a well earned professional version and the second place!

 

 

Platz 3...

TYRANNIZER, came third and is by Viper Byte.


This you-know-what clone (sigh) is winner in the categories best graphics and best topography.

Nothing can be criticised about the graphics, they simply are a shining example indeed. They additionally were well executed with an obvious touch for architecture, producing a high quality topography instantly creating athmosphere.

               
And for nitpickers: concerning the highly praised graphics the opponents are admittedly a bit poor compared to the level.  Programming them was done without investing too much care. But we are confident that this will change - as will the sounds, which are completly missing as of now.


We'll be following the progress with great interest and for now congratulate them for coming in third!

If you have read our short reviews with attention you know according to which criteria we decide upon our ranking. Idea and execution are the thing! It's like real life: what good is an idea for the painting of the century, if you think that magenta is some kind of metal and you only use brushes to brush your hair. Or else: what good is being adept at handling colours and brushes, when all you ever paint is grandma Wilma's gross poodle. Could hardly be called a creative genius, could you?
