Interviews with ACKNEX Users


 
 
Our second interview we conducted via e-mail with the producers of the political adventure game "The Name of the Brother". Our first question was: who are you and why did you do this game? Here's the answer:   

As a result of the increasing availability of right-wing radical computer games at the beginning of the 90s, at the TU Vienna we (a group of computer sciences students) developed the concept of an alternative anti-fascist game. After implementing a prototype on the Amiga system (in those days still with 2D technology a la Monkey Island or Kings Quest) we for a long time went looking for sponsors for the project. After 2 years we finally succeeded in getting the project partly financed and started developing the game in its present form using the ACKNEX engine.   

Thank you for introducing yourselves. 

AUM: Why did you decide to use a 3D action environment for your political, strategic game? One of the usual adventure engines or an isometric view would probably have been more suitable?   

NdB: We couldn't find a serviceable engine for 2d or isometric graphics. And we couldn't programm it ourselves because our resources were pretty tight. . 

AUM:. How long have you been working on NoB?? 

NdB:we started developing the concept about four or five years ago. The current form (3d graphics) we have been working on for almost 2 years now.  

AUM: And with how many people on the team (commercial artists, musicians, programmers)? 

NdB: There are 3 programmers, and world designers 
1 writing dialogue, developing simulations and organising,  
2 commercial artists   
1 architect (level design)   
1 sound designer and musician   
7 testers 

AUM: Which way do you intend to publish the game? Is there a marketing plan? Or a shareware distributor?    

NdB: A club has taken over distributing the game, where you can order it  at a price covering the costs. Apart from that the game will be distributed via youth clubs. We have contacts that allow us to place ads and announcements in several media free of charge.    

AUM: After deciding to do a 3d game - did you also try out other 3D toolkits before deciding in favour of ACKNEX?    

NdB: We looked at anything we could get a hold on. But ACKNEX was the only engine that to us appears to be up to the task of developing such a complex game.    

AUM: What do you like about  ACKNEX?  

NdB: The graphics and performance are quite good. There's a fast scripting language (extremely important to us, because we had to do a LOT of programming). A developing environment (Interpreter and Level Editor), good support (new versions and bugfixes always arrived pretty fast) . 

AUM: We'll certainly pass that on to the developers. What would you like to see changed?  

NbD: A better programming language with dynamic structures (Arrays, Lists) and more high-level language (loops, functions, etc.) and real object orientation (inheritance, object trees). A debugger (watching the variables during runtime etc.). An improved world editor (better editing of waypoints, creating object definitions in the WED, something like Visual Basic) .  

AUM: Which additional tools do you use (sounds, editors, graphics tools)? 

NdB: Adobe Photoshop, DeluxePaint, Aldus Photostyler, Paintshop Pro (graphics) qMe (Quake Models) BorlandC, PFE (Editor) CoolEdit (Sound) and a pre-processor we developed ourselves, which translates a high-level language (highly expanded WDL) into WDL. This language includes WHILE, SWITCH-CASE, functions, dialogue, support for inventory (objects surviving a change of levels) etc.   

AUM: What are your favourite games (all over and 3D)??  

NdB: MDK, Earthworm Jim (Shine) Ultima Serie, System Shock (Origin) Kings Quest, Space Quest (Sierra) Quake (id) Descent  

AUM: How did you get the idea of doing an anti-fascist game of all things? Where you inspired by Wolfenstein ?   

NdB: In the 90s right wing radical games kept popping up more and more, there was the series of letter bombs in Austria, Haider's rise and a general move towards the right in Austria and Europe. With our game we wanted to make clear the mechanisms and consequences of fascism and dictatorship to the young people. And because of that Castle Wolfenstein wasn't really an inspiration, more like the contrary.   

AUM: What do you expect from the game? Is it not probably the case that you will be preaching to the converted, i.e. that only those will be playing the game, who are on your wavelength anyhow?     

NdB: We believe that our game should be marketed as a good adventure game (graphics, idea, plot) and also be perceived as such. Our announcements take heed of that (we make a point of not writing "anti-fascist game").   

AUM: And how do you like what you have produced so far? 

NdB: Probably better ask again in a year ,-)   

AUM:  Would program another game again with ACKNEX?   

NdB: We've already been thinking about a game to follow up this one. (But we won't be telling any more right now)    

AUM:: Well, obviously you're not telling anything at all ;-) What do you think about the previously released ACKNEX games you know about?  

NdB: We bought Skaphander and Angst (get to know your competitors). 
Angst: lousy plot, lousy graphics, lousy idea (fad)   
Skaphander: nice idea, good graphics, today's engine could do a lot more.  

AUM:  What are your whishes for the future? Have you smelled blood and would like to get ahead in the game business? 

That this game will finally be complete.   
Holiday.   
Whose head?   

AUM: Well thanks a lot for the head....the interview! And good luck to you all!
