Welcome

to the second 1998 edition! Just because it's July right now does not mean that the magazine is published semi-annually. We had a few problems and restructurings. But right now  we are confident that we'll be able to slowly step up the frequency of editions.

Our former editor, Reinhard Wissdorf, has left us and I'm here to fill the gap. My name is Felicitas Oehler and I am familiar with Game Studio 3D through my own, painful experience. And that is why I understand the immediate need for such a magazine. Even though I'd rather be lying at the poolside or walking the dog. Instead I'm here sitting at my computer, toiling away, battling the editor. BTW: greetings from Reinhard. He's probably enjyong a swim right now...

First thing that might strike you is the new layout. We aimed to attain good readability and better perspicuity. This we believe we did. Do write us to let us know, what you think! 

The screenshots no longer are necessarily from the game to be reviewed in the upcoming edition. Here we simply offer a gallery of shots from any which game or even only level created with the engine and looking good. Which means that we are not only open to receive your entries - we invite you to send in your suggestions. 

The HINTS-Page will henceforth be attended to by George Dan Pirvu. He never tires to pry the last secretes out of the engine to inform us about them. Which does not mean that you no longer can take the floor. Whoever discovers anything about Game Studio 3D and wishes to tell the world about it, is invited to contribute. Just like M. (Matthias, Mike, Michael?) Bhatty did this time.

And finally allow me to introduce two new colleagues:

BERKELEY

and

CANDELABER

Berkeley is a rather optimistic fellow and is always happy to see things well done. Result: high spirits and a tendency to turn a blind eye on faults. 

  

He plays in a totaly different league from his joyful colleague: Candelaber is a true groucho. He is bound to find each fly in the WDL-ointment and remorselessly drags it into the light of day. 
 

It may not always be easy with them, but once you know how to take them, their a perfect match.

What else? Enjoy the edition! 
And happy WDLing!

Sincerely Felicitas Oehler
